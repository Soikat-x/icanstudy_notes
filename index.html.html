Effective Planning & Goal Setting

The Problem: Planning Ineffectively

  • Many people, regardless of age or experience, don't plan very effectively.
  • You might know SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Realistic, Time-bound). These are good basic guidelines, especially if you're new to goal setting.
  • However, often these aren't enough to create a truly successful plan.

The Big Difference: Goal Setting vs. Intention Setting

This is a crucial distinction. They are two completely different things:

1. Intention Setting (What most people do):

  • This is simply saying, "I want to achieve this thing," or "I'll try to do it."
  • It feels good and motivational. You might make a poster or remind yourself often.
  • The Downside: It lacks a concrete plan. Most people have intentions, not actual goals with actionable steps. It doesn't detail how you'll achieve the outcome.
    • For example, students might say their goal is "top 1% grade," but their plan is just "study really hard" or "try my best." When asked how or with what, the details are often missing.

2. Effective Goal Setting (The Aim):

  • This means creating a "High Propensity Plan" – a plan optimized for success, with the highest chance of achieving the goal.
  • It involves thinking through the actual tasks and actionables that will turn the intention into reality.
  • This is more difficult and complex to think through initially and can feel overwhelming.
  • It's not just about age or maturity; even professionals can fall into "intention setting."

The Way Forward:

  • The goal is to move beyond just intentions.
  • We need a strategic and systematic way to go through goal setting.
  • The process you'll learn can be used as a template for any future goal you have, especially complex or overwhelming ones where you want to ensure you hit the mark.
